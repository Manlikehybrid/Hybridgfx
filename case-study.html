01 — Overview

THE BRIEF

Aura Wellness is a concept wellness brand built as part of my portfolio to demonstrate my full brand identity capability. The challenge: create a brand that feels premium yet approachable — something that could sit next to The Ordinary or Glossier on a shelf and not look out of place.

Most wellness brands choose between two camps: clinical white-and-green minimalism, or loud bohemian earth tones. I wanted Aura to occupy a third space — sophisticated, spiritual, and modern simultaneously.

The deliverables included a full logo suite, brand guidelines document, packaging mockups (serum bottles, box packaging), social media post templates, and a complete visual language guide.

02 — The Challenge

PROBLEM & SOLUTION

The Problem
Generic wellness brands look the same

The wellness market is saturated. Logos with leaves, sage green color palettes, and Playfair Display headlines are everywhere. A new brand needs a visual identity that breaks this mold while still feeling trustworthy and premium. The risk: go too far and it stops feeling like wellness. Don't go far enough and it disappears into the crowd.

The Solution
A brand built around mystique and clarity

I anchored Aura in deep purple and warm off-white — a pairing that feels luxurious without being cold. The logo uses a geometric mark inspired by light refraction (the "aura" effect) rather than predictable botanicals. The typography pairs a geometric sans with a refined serif to balance modernity and warmth. Every touchpoint is intentional.

03 — Process

HOW IT WAS BUILT

01
Research & Moodboarding

I spent two days studying competitors — Goop, Ceremonia, Tatcha, The Ordinary — mapping what they do visually and identifying the whitespace. I built a moodboard focused on light, aura photography, crystal aesthetics, and high-end cosmetic packaging to set the visual direction.

02
Logo Exploration (CorelDRAW)

I sketched 12 rough concepts by hand, shortlisted 4, and executed 3 full versions in CorelDRAW. The final mark is a geometric radial form — concentric shapes that suggest an "aura" emanating outward, balanced with clean wordmark typography. I tested it at every size from favicon to billboard.

03
Color & Type System

Color selection was the most critical decision. I tested 18 palette variations before landing on the final system. Typography followed — the combination needed to work across packaging (small, print), digital (screen), and social (cropped, fast-scrolling). Every pairing was stress-tested in real contexts.

04
Packaging & Mockups (Photoshop)

The brand came to life in Photoshop — serum bottles, outer box packaging, and glass jar mockups. I used smart object templates, applied the full brand system, and photographed (digitally) from multiple angles. The goal was to make concept work feel like a real product launch.

05
Social Media Kit

I built 12 social media templates in CorelDRAW — square posts, story formats, highlight covers, and a profile grid layout. Each template maintains brand consistency while being flexible enough for different content types: product shots, quotes, promotions, and announcements.

04 — Identity System

VISUAL IDENTITY

The Aura mark is built on a radial geometry system — 5 concentric shapes that grow outward from the center, representing energy radiating from within. The proportions are mathematically precise; each ring follows a 1.4× scale ratio. The wordmark sits below in a custom-spaced geometric sans, with a ligature between the A and U that reinforces the brand's uniqueness.

The logo works in three primary configurations: stacked (primary), horizontal (secondary), and icon-only (favicon/avatar use). All versions are available in full-color, single-color, and reversed variants.

08 — Takeaways

WHAT I LEARNED

Every project teaches something. These are the key takeaways from building Aura — things I apply to every branding project now:

  • Color decisions take longer than logo decisions. The palette is what clients notice first and remember longest. Don't rush it — test it in real contexts across print, screen, and photography before committing.
  • Concept projects need the same rigor as real ones. A portfolio piece that looks rushed is worse than not having it at all. Treat every concept like a paying client hired you.
  • The logo is only 20% of a brand. The real brand lives in how typography, color, space, and voice work together across every touchpoint. Packaging and social templates make a concept feel real.
  • Stress-test at every size. A mark that looks stunning at A3 can fall apart at 32px favicon size. Test early, test often — scale up and down throughout the process, not just at the end.
  • Research before design, always. The two days I spent on competitive analysis saved me from creating something generic. Understanding what already exists is the fastest way to find what doesn't.
